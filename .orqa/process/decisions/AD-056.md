# Ethical Licensing Model

## Decision

OrqaStudio uses a tiered licensing model designed to keep the tool free for people and causes that serve equal opportunity, while preventing exploitation by entities whose goals conflict with the project's values.

## Context

OrqaStudio is built to democratise AI-assisted structured thinking — making it accessible to individuals, small teams, charities, and non-profits who serve the public good. The licensing must:

1. Keep the source open and readable
2. Allow free personal use
3. Allow free use by aligned charities and non-profits
4. Prevent corporations from hosting it as a commercial service and profiting from the work
5. Prevent use by organisations that actively harm equal opportunity, healthcare access, education access, or democratic participation
6. Convert to a fully open license after a rolling 4-year window
7. Welcome contributions from anyone who shares the philosophy

## The License

**BSL-1.1 (Business Source License 1.1)** for all core components, with Additional Use Grants.

### Why BSL-1.1

- Source is publicly visible — anyone can read, learn from, and modify the code
- Non-production use is unrestricted (development, testing, education, research)
- Production use is restricted for 4 years from each release, then converts to Apache 2.0
- The Additional Use Grant mechanism allows carve-outs for aligned users without changing the license text

### Change Date

4 years rolling from each release. Every tagged release starts its own 4-year clock.

### Change License

After the change date, each release converts to **Apache 2.0 with an Ethical Use Addendum** (see `CHANGE-LICENSE` in every repo). This is a custom hybrid:

- **Apache 2.0 freedoms** — use, modify, distribute, sublicense, commercial use
- **Permanent ethical restriction** — the Prohibited Purpose clause (protected characteristics, SaaS hosting) survives the change date indefinitely
- **Not OSI-approved** — acknowledged openly. The addendum is the price of permanent ethical protection. We believe this is the right tradeoff.

This means the code becomes effectively open-source after 4 years — anyone can use, modify, and distribute it — except organisations that actively harm marginalised communities. That restriction never expires.

### Additional Use Grants

The following uses are granted immediately, without waiting for the change date:

1. **Personal use** — any individual using OrqaStudio for their own work, learning, or projects
2. **Aligned charities and non-profits** — organisations whose mission serves:
   - Equal access to healthcare, education, housing, or emergency services
   - Democratisation of technology and AI
   - Environmental sustainability
   - Social mobility and equal opportunity
   - Community empowerment and civic participation
3. **Open-source projects** — projects that are themselves open-source and serve the public good
4. **Educational institutions** — schools, universities, and training programmes

### Prohibited Purposes

The following are explicitly defined as Prohibited Purposes — no production use grant applies:

1. **Commercial SaaS hosting** — no entity may host OrqaStudio (or a derivative) as a paid hosted or managed service
2. **Use by organisations that restrict human rights** — any organisation whose mission, activities, or funded advocacy actively works to restrict, diminish, or remove the rights of any person or group based on race, ethnicity, national origin, sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, or socioeconomic status. This explicitly includes organisations that fund, lobby for, or promote policies that deny equal access to healthcare, education, housing, or legal protections on any of these grounds.

Gender identity and gender expression are named specifically and deliberately. Trans rights are human rights. This project will not be a tool used to take them away.

### Tier Table

| Component | License | Rationale |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| App | BSL-1.1 | Core product |
| Libraries | BSL-1.1 | Shared code is product infrastructure |
| Plugins (first-party) | BSL-1.1 | Official extensions |
| Connectors | Apache-2.0 | Third-party tool bridges need permissive licensing for compatibility |
| Tools | MIT | Dev utilities freely usable |
| Registries | MIT | Community infrastructure must be open |

## Contributor Model

**DCO (Developer Certificate of Origin)** — contributors sign off on commits (`Signed-off-by:` line) confirming they have the right to submit the code and agree to the project's license. No CLA required.

Contributors retain copyright of their contributions. The project license applies to the combined work.

### Who Can Contribute

Anyone who shares the project's philosophy: AI should be a tool that builds a society where everyone is looked after and treated equally. Quality of life measured by disposable income per capita, not GDP. Entrepreneurship and competitive innovation are valued, but with regulations ensuring the baseline is equal opportunity, not mere survival.

## Sponsorship

The project welcomes sponsorship from aligned organisations but will not grant exclusive control, governance, or licensing modifications to any single sponsor. Independence is a core principle — beholden to the mission, not to any corporate entity.

## Consequences

- Source is always readable — transparency is non-negotiable
- Personal users and aligned non-profits can use it immediately and freely
- Corporations must wait 4 years or negotiate a commercial license
- No cloud hosting by third parties for profit
- Every release eventually becomes fully open (Apache 2.0)
- The project remains independent and mission-driven
