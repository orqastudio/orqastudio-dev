## Decision

OrqaStudio's mission statement evolved through three stages:

1. **Initial** (2026-03-02): "Forge" — a VS Code-like development environment for AI-assisted coding with governance
2. **Rebrand** (2026-03-04): "Orqa Studio" — rebranded with custom identity, `src/` → `ui/`, `.forge/` → project-level config
3. **Vision evolution** (2026-03-07): "AI-assisted clarity engine" — reframed as domain-agnostic, broadening from software development to any field requiring structured thinking

The pillars were renamed to reflect universality:
- "Process Governance" → **"Clarity Through Structure"** — making thinking, standards, and decisions visible
- "Self-Learning Loop" → **"Learning Through Reflection"** — improving over time through structured retrospection

Software development remains the first domain, not the only one. The governance framework (agents, rules, skills, lessons) applies to any domain where complex work benefits from structured process.

## Rationale

The original "Forge" framing was too narrow — it positioned the product as a developer tool competing with VS Code, Cursor, and Windsurf. The actual value proposition is the governance layer: making process visible, enforceable, and self-improving. This applies equally to product management, research, operations, and any knowledge work that benefits from structured thinking and continuous improvement.

The pillar renaming made the framework portable. "Process Governance" sounds corporate and software-specific. "Clarity Through Structure" describes a universal human need.

## Consequences

- All documentation, UI labels, and marketing materials use the clarity engine framing
- Software development terminology in the UI is replaced with domain-neutral language where possible
- The governance framework is designed to be transportable (future: `.orqa/` as a portable format)
- Architecture decisions serve the broader mission, not just software development
- Claude is the current AI provider, not the product identity (see [AD-ff88ecea](AD-ff88ecea))
