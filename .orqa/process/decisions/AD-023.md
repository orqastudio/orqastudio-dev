## Decision

The "Plan" artifact type is deprecated and removed from the artifact framework. All existing plan files are migrated to `.orqa/delivery/research/` and marked `status: surpassed`. Going forward:

- **Research** documents cover all investigative work: investigations, design explorations, architecture spikes, and what were previously "implementation plans"
- **Epics** contain their implementation design directly in the markdown body (data model, IPC contracts, component breakdown, approach)
- **Tasks** always reference an epic via the `epic:` field (never `plan:`)
- **Epics** may reference research via `research-refs:` array

The traceability chain is: **Task → Epic → Milestone**, with research linked via `research-refs` wherever relevant.

## Rationale

Plans and research documents had significant overlap — both were investigation artifacts that informed implementation. The distinction ("research is investigation, plans are commitment") was unclear in practice, and the `plan:` field on tasks broke the Task → Epic → Milestone chain. Agents referencing plans instead of epics created orphaned traceability paths.

Merging plans into research eliminates the overlap and establishes a single clear hierarchy:
- Research = thinking (investigations, explorations, designs — may be surpassed)
- Epics = commitment (approved work with design in body — tracks to milestone)
- Tasks = execution (specific deliverables — always traces to an epic)

## Consequences

- No `plan:` field on any artifact frontmatter
- No `.orqa/delivery/plans/` directory
- No Plan type in artifact-framework.md
- No plan status transitions in artifact-lifecycle.md
- Epics are richer documents — their body is the implementation spec
- Research category field supports: `investigation`, `design`, `spike`, `plan`
- The `plan-mode-compliance` rule still exists but governs the *activity* of planning, not an artifact type
